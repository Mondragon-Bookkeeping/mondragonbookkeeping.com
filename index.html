Sound Familiar?

The questions that keep
business owners up at night

These aren't hypothetical. They're the exact things I hear from almost every new client before we start working together.

"I have no idea what I owe until April."
Stop Guessing Most people find out what they owe in April — sometimes years after they could have done something about it. Quarterly planning means no surprises and no scrambling.
"My books are somewhere. Probably fine."
Messy Books Cost More Than You Think Missed deductions, inaccurate reports, stress at filing time — it all traces back to disorganized books. Clean records aren't just tidy. They're your first line of defense.
"My accountant just files my return. That's it."
Reactive ≠ Helpful Filing your return is the bare minimum. The strategies that actually reduce your tax bill — entity structure, retirement timing, deduction planning — only work if someone brings them to you before December 31st.
"I spend hours every week on admin."
Your Time Has a Dollar Value Every hour you spend on bookkeeping, invoicing, or payroll is an hour you're not running your business. At some point, doing it yourself costs more than getting help.
"Am I even structured the right way?"
This Is One of the Biggest Decisions You'll Make The wrong business entity can cost you tens of thousands in unnecessary self-employment tax every single year. Most people don't know — because no one told them to ask.
"Is my business actually profitable?"
Revenue Isn't the Same as Profit Without clean monthly financials, every decision you make — pricing, hiring, spending — is a guess. You can't manage what you can't see.

About Brandon
"The best tax work happens before the deadline. Most people find that out too late."

I built this practice around a problem I've seen my entire life.

Growing up, everyone around me worked hard. Family members, friends, neighbors worked 10, 12 hours a day for years. Immigrant families, first-generation entrepreneurs, people running their own businesses out of nothing. And almost universally, the same result: working endlessly, barely getting ahead.

I used to think that was just how it was.

After becoming an accountant, I saw clearly for the first time that the problem wasn't effort — it was the absence of real financial guidance. Not a tax preparer who files a return and disappears. Actual guidance. The kind where someone sits down with you, understands your specific situation, and helps you make decisions that compound over time — lower tax liability, proper structure, retirement built while you're still earning.

That gap is what I built this practice for.

I'm Brandon Mondragon. I'm a first-generation college graduate, a solo practitioner based in San Diego, and the founder of Mondragon Tax & Advisory. I serve individuals, business owners, rental property owners, and investors — and I handle every file personally. When you work with me, you're always talking directly to me.

My approach is simple: I take the time to understand your situation deeply, I communicate everything clearly, and I stay in your corner year-round — not just at tax time. My fees are higher because the level of care is higher. Every client leaves knowing exactly what happened, why, and what to do next.

Advisory-Level Tax Preparation

Your return, handled with care and precision

This isn't a drop-off-and-file service. Every return I prepare includes a personalized advisory package — specific notes, findings, and action steps tailored to your situation. You'll understand exactly what was filed and why, plus what you can do differently going forward.

I don't skip over details that might affect you, and I don't mention things that don't apply. What you get is a thorough, precise review of your tax picture — and a practitioner who will follow through with you throughout the year.

How pricing works
Every return starts with a free consultation where we review your situation together. Pricing is based on the forms, schedules, and depth of work your return requires — the more moving parts, the more time and care it takes to do it right. A single W-2 return starts at $500. Your exact quote is provided before we begin, so there are no surprises.

everyone deserves real guidance.

These aren't marketing slogans. They're the commitments behind every client interaction — and the reason this practice exists.

01
Clarity over complexity
Tax and accounting are complex. Our job is to make sure you never feel that complexity. Every memo, every call — the most important thing is said first, and said plainly.
02
Precision without pretense
We triple-check everything. We own our mistakes. We say "I don't know, but I'll find out" instead of guessing. Accuracy matters more than the appearance of confidence.

Always improving
Tax law changes. Technology changes. We change with it. We stay current, bring every relevant update to clients before they have to ask, and are always honest about what we're still learning.
